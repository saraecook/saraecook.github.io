A little bit about me

I am passionate about education and adult learning.

Like many, my career has had twists and turns, and for this I am thankful. Each position I have held has given me the opportunity to develop different skills and perspective, which has shaped me into the person and educator I am today.


In my current role at the University of Connecticut I plan, prioritize, and create complex trainings through written instruction, classroom teaching, advising meetings, and experiential learning. Specifically, I plan, design, and create an average of six learning experiences a semester. This includes a written curriculum for the learners, and facilitation notes for myself or other facilitators. I also collaborate with a team of 10 other staff members to plan and create a year-long leadership development program for 110 students. One of the many highlights of my current job is developing content and then watching it play out through implementation.


As a life-long learner, I recently earned a Masters degree in Adult Learning. Through this process I have learned how to interpret the different ways adults learn, retain, and apply new information. This has assisted me in finding the best methods to construct learning for targeted audiences, so that the learner will feel comfortable applying the knew knowledge and skills to their unique environments.


My Philosophy and Guiding Principles for Facilitating Life-Long Learning:


  • Provide opportunity for the learners to become aware of their true self, and values.
  • Create a supportive, safe, group environment free of judgement and pressure.
  • Motivate learners through relevant, real-life, relatable content and reinforcement that postulates meaning to the learner.
  • Support and take positive risks
  • Construct new knowledge together with a community of like minded learners.

Why Github?

"Github is filled with amazing people and has fostered an incredible culture"

In addition to recently earning my degree, I also have been learning how to code, including the basics of Git and GitHub. It is through this that I discovered the job opening with GitHub.


I love the idea that GitHub has a large distributed team working all over the world, and the fact they are able to create and ship amazing products consistently.

One of my personal goals is to be able to have the flexibility and freedom to work remotely so that I have the time to spend with my family and enjoy life. Reading through Github's website it is apparent that GitHub is filled with amazing people and fosters an incredible culture, one in which I want to be apart of.
